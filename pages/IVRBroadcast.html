What Is the Purpose of Voice Broadcasting?

Voice Broadcasting allows you to simultaneously broadcast recorded voice notes, text messages, and emails to numerous phone lines. You may either pre-record a message or make a voice message in the interface and send it immediately or on a schedule. The steps to set up voice broadcasting are as follows:

  • Fill out your contact information in the system, or import your whole contact list in Excel or CSV file.
  • Using your phone/laptop, record the message to be transmitted, or upload the voice note you want to broadcast.
  • You may arrange when and to whom your voice message is transmitted when it is uploaded to the system. You may obtain a complete report on the progress and outcome of each phone conversation once it's been planned and dispatched.

How does voice broadcasting work?

To start a voice broadcasting campaign, just register as a client and complete these simple steps:

  • Upload phone numbers of recipients to our protected website.
  • Make audio recordings of your outgoing messages by dialing the toll-free number provided.
  • Start calling from the website or a toll-free number (premium option). The number and length of phone listings you offer are unrestricted. Lists should be in a.csv format (a common Excel text format). If you wish, we may record your messages professionally.
  • The phone systems start making calls as soon as you start the voice broadcast campaign. Your message is played if someone answers the phone (or if our phone system detects an answering machine). If your call is not answered, the phone system will attempt thrice to relay your message later.

Dialer

IVR

This feature allows callers to participate in the conversation, express their ideas, and receive messages based on the number they press on the keypad. IVR (interactive voice response) is a feature of an automated company phone system that interacts with callers and collects information by providing them with menu options. The system then takes action based on the caller's keypad or voice response responses. The IVR's activities are determined by the caller's choices: it can deliver information or, if the situation is more complicated, it can direct callers to a human agent who can better address their requirements.
