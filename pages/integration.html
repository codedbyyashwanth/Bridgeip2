Customer relationship management (CRM) refers to the methods, tactics, and technology that businesses employ to manage and analyze customer interactions and data throughout the lifespan of their customers.

CRM aims to strengthen corporate connections, aid in customer retention, and drive sales growth - all while evaluating consumers' personal data, purchase history, purchasing preferences, and concerns.

CRM systems collect information on customers from various mediums – or points of contact between the consumer and the firm – such as the firm's website, social networks, live chat, direct mail, marketing materials, and telephone.
